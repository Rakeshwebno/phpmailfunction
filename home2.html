How the Backseat-Trader algorithm works

It analyses all stock trading on the NASDAQ index

From there it selects the Top 20 to trade on a monthly rotation

Each month the algorithm updates the Top 20 for the coming month

Selecting the Top 20 provides diversification within the portfolio

This diversification reduces risk exposure whilst targeting a net-profit of 15 – 20%

Backtesting

Feasibility Analysis 27% Annualised Return

This summary page presents an outline of the approach Cosmic Algorithm Ltd (CAL) undertook when developing the Backseat-Trader algorithm.

The chart on the left is a comparison between the algorithm's historical equity curve versus the value of SPY, QQQ, and SPY-AGG 70/30 portfolio equity curves. All systems are rebalanced monthly with US$10,000 initial investment. There is no capital reinvestment assumed.

CAL can supply the back-testing data used in electronic format, upon request.

A momentum-based semi-automated stock rotation system. Only long positions are traded. The system tracks NASDAQ index components on a daily basis and selects the top 20 best performing stocks of the index. The selection is rebalanced once per month which increases return and reduces volatility

CAL does summarily list the analytical methodologies for this algorithm:

  1. Runs on daily candles
  2. Absolute momentum quantification mechanism based on 13612 weighted filter
  3. Relative momentum ranking and weight allocation system
  4. Support for stock and ETF portfolios
  5. Support for leveraged and unleveraged accounts
  6. Adjustable rebalance period
